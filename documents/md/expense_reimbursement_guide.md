# Expense Reimbursement Guide

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Document Version:** 2.0

---

## 1. Overview

This guide provides specific procedures and guidelines for submitting and processing expense reimbursements. All employees are expected to follow these procedures to ensure timely processing of claims.

---

## 2. Eligible Expenses

### 2.1 Business Travel
- Airfare (economy class only)
- Hotel accommodation (up to $200/night)
- Rental car (mid-size or smaller)
- Taxi, rideshare, public transport
- Parking and tolls
- Fuel (for personal vehicle usage)

### 2.2 Meals During Travel
- Breakfast: up to $15
- Lunch: up to $18
- Dinner: up to $30
- Total daily maximum: $60
- Alcohol: up to $10 per meal if business-related

### 2.3 Conference and Training
- Registration fees
- Training materials and books
- Online course subscriptions
- Professional certification exam fees
- Travel and accommodation to conferences

### 2.4 Client Entertainment
- Client meals (up to $75 per person)
- Theater or sporting event tickets (business purpose)
- Golf or recreational activities (with client)
- Gratuity: 15-20% of bill

### 2.5 Office Supplies
- Stationery and pens
- Notebooks and folders
- Printer ink and paper
- Batteries and charging cables
- Desk organizers and storage

### 2.6 Communications
- Mobile phone bills (business portion only)
- Internet service (work-from-home)
- Video conferencing equipment
- Subscription software

---

## 3. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

### 3.1 Prohibited Items
- Personal grooming and hygiene products
- Entertainment (movies, books, magazines)
- Gym memberships
- Parking violations or traffic fines
- Alcoholic beverages outside of client entertainment
- Personal travel insurance
- Home furniture and appliances
- Medical or dental expenses

### 3.2 Personal Development
- Clothing and accessories
- Haircuts and spa treatments
- Personal fitness items
- Home office furniture (desks, chairs)
- Internet for personal use

---

## 4. Receipt and Documentation Requirements

### 4.1 Original Receipts Required
- All expenses $25 and above require receipt
- Expenses under $25: Manager approval only (no receipt)
- Digital/email receipts accepted
- Original receipts must be kept for 7 years

### 4.2 Receipt Content
Must include:
- Vendor name and date
- Description of items purchased
- Amount and currency
- Payment method
- Itemized breakdown (not just total)

### 4.3 Missing Receipts
- Affidavit form required
- Manager and HR approval needed
- Only for amounts under $50
- Maximum 2 per quarter

---

## 5. Submission Process

### 5.1 Online Submission
1. Log into Expense Management System (EMS)
2. Create new expense report
3. Enter trip dates and business purpose
4. Upload all receipts (PDF, JPG, PNG)
5. Categorize each expense
6. Add manager comment/approval reason
7. Submit for manager review

### 5.2 Manager Review
- Manager approves/rejects within 5 business days
- Can request additional documentation
- Sends to Finance for processing

### 5.3 Finance Verification
- Verify receipt validity
- Check policy compliance
- Validate amounts
- Process reimbursement

---

## 6. Reimbursement Timeline

### 6.1 Processing Schedule
- Day 1-2: Employee submits report
- Day 3-5: Manager reviews and approves
- Day 6-10: Finance processes
- Day 11-15: Direct deposit to employee account

### 6.2 Delays
- Missing receipts: Hold for 10 days, then request
- Policy violations: Return to employee for clarification
- Incomplete information: Email sent to employee

---

## 7. Travel Expense Guidelines

### 7.1 Airfare Rules
- Book minimum 2 weeks in advance
- Use corporate travel portal when available
- Economy class only (exceptions: flights over 10 hours)
- No premium economy or business class
- Air miles must be used when available

### 7.2 Hotel Accommodation
- Use corporate hotel partners for discounts
- Maximum $200/night (including taxes)
- 4-star hotels maximum
- No resort fees covered
- Breakfast must be included in rate when possible

### 7.3 Ground Transportation
- Rental car: Compact or mid-size only
- No luxury vehicles (no BMW, Mercedes, Cadillac)
- Insurance: Use company policy, no upgrade
- Rideshare: Uber/Lyft acceptable for short trips
- Public transportation: Preferred when available

### 7.4 Incidental Expenses
- Baggage fees: $35 maximum per trip
- Taxi tips: 15-20%
- Parking: Daily maximum $30
- Valet parking: Not covered (use self-parking)

---

## 8. Corporate Credit Card

### 8.1 Company Card Usage
- Issued to approved employees
- For business expenses only
- Personal use prohibited
- Monthly statement reconciliation required

### 8.2 Reconciliation
- Match receipts to card statement
- Note any missing items
- Attach receipts to monthly report
- Submit within 5 days of month-end

### 8.3 Non-Compliant Charges
- Will be requested for immediate payment
- May result in card suspension
- Repeated violations may lead to disciplinary action

---

## 9. Mileage Reimbursement

### 9.1 Personal Vehicle Usage
- Rate: $0.67 per mile (updated annually)
- Use when travel outside company vehicle policy
- Requires manager pre-approval

### 9.2 Calculation
- Actual business miles only
- Start/end location documentation required
- MapQuest or Google Maps for distance verification
- Personal commute not eligible

---

## 10. Meal Reimbursement Details

### 10.1 Meal Per Diems
- **Breakfast:** $15 maximum
- **Lunch:** $18 maximum
- **Dinner:** $30 maximum
- **Daily total:** $60 maximum

### 10.2 Working Meals
- Team lunch during meeting: Full amount reimbursable
- Client meals: Up to $75 per person
- Employee meal with client meal: Employee portion capped at daily limits

### 10.3 Alcohol Policy
- Alcoholic beverages: Only with clients
- Beer/Wine: Up to $10 per meal
- Spirits: $15 maximum per meal
- Non-alcoholic beverages: Always reimbursable

---

## 11. Approval Authorities

### 11.1 Approval Levels
- **Manager:** Up to $500
- **Department Head:** $501 to $2,500
- **CFO:** $2,501 to $10,000
- **CEO:** Over $10,000

### 11.2 Violations and Overages
- Expenses exceeding limits: Rejected by default
- Manager can override with written justification
- Requires next-level approval

---

## 12. Currency and Exchange

### 12.1 International Expenses
- Reimbursement in USD at date of transaction
- Receipt must show original currency amount
- Include exchange rate if manually calculated
- Company card handles automatic conversion

### 12.2 Currency Conversion
- Bank exchange rate used for verification
- Credit card rate applied to card charges
- X-rate.com used for manual calculations
- Transaction date rate applies

---

## 13. Tax Implications

### 13.1 Tax-Free Reimbursement
- Compliant expenses are tax-free
- Non-compliant expenses taxable to employee
- Annual summary provided for tax filing
- Meal and entertainment expenses may be taxable

### 13.2 Accountable Plan
- Company maintains accountable plan
- Substantiation required within 60 days
- Excess reimbursement subject to withholding
- Employees responsible for excess taxes

---

## 14. Common Mistakes

### 14.1 Submission Errors
- Missing original receipt (most common)
- Unclear business purpose
- Wrong expense category
- Incomplete itemization

### 14.2 Policy Violations
- Exceeding daily meal limits
- Premium hotel brands
- Business class airfare without approval
- Personal items mixed with business

---

## 15. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How long do I have to submit expenses?
**A:** 30 days from transaction date. Late submissions may be rejected.

### Q: What if I don't have a receipt?
**A:** For $25+, receipt required. Under $25 requires manager approval only.

### Q: Can I be reimbursed for my spouse's meal during travel?
**A:** Only if spouse attended business meeting. Provide explanation.

### Q: Are personal meals on travel days covered?
**A:** Yes, up to daily limits: $15 breakfast, $18 lunch, $30 dinner.

### Q: What about tips and gratuities?
**A:** Tips 15-20% of pre-tax bill are reimbursable for business meals.
